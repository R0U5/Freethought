My NPC Co-Worker

A work-anniversary “congrats” that sounded like a template made me realize how fast we’re trading individuality for convenience.

By William Furtado November 3, 2025 4 min read

One day, after four long years of dedication to my job, I received the standard flood of “Happy Anniversary" posts, spurred on by a gentle email reminder from HR to the team. While most were genuine or, at the very least, apathetic to the celebration, one stood out to me amongst the others. It said: “Congrats on your 4th Anniversary — that’s quite a feat!”.

Aside from the arguably passive-aggressive tone (why is it a feat?!!), what struck me the most was that naughty little em-dash being used. A telltale sign of GPT4/5 and a glaring signal that this person actually chose typing more to prompt the GPT for a message to congratulate me, than to actually just write "congrats!".

At that moment, I began to understand that a large group of society would prioritize convenience over individuality, even at the most benign levels. While I wanted to say something, I did not out of embarrassment for us both, as this is essentially the social equivalent of getting a Dollar Store pack of Pokémon cards for a Secret Santa Gift.

If one wonders why the rise of the "NPC" trend is so prevalent nowadays, one only needs to look at the trade-off happening in our daily lives between the ever-perilous balance of convenience vs humanity.
